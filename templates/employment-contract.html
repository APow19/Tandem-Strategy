The Employee will be paid a salary of £{{ANNUAL_SALARY}} per annum, payable in equal monthly instalments in arrears on the {{PAY_DATE}} of each month by BACS transfer to the Employee's nominated bank account.

The Employer operates an auto-enrolment pension scheme in compliance with the Pensions Act 2008. Subject to eligibility criteria, the Employee will be automatically enrolled into the pension scheme. Details of the scheme, including employer and employee contribution rates, will be provided separately. The Employee has the right to opt out of the scheme.

The Employee is entitled to {{HOLIDAY_DAYS}} days' paid annual leave per leave year (inclusive of bank and public holidays), accruing at the rate of {{HOLIDAY_ACCRUAL_RATE}} days per month of completed service. The leave year runs from {{LEAVE_YEAR_START}} to {{LEAVE_YEAR_END}}.

Annual leave must be taken in agreement with the Employer and must not be carried over into the following leave year except in cases of illness or statutory leave, unless expressly agreed in writing by the Employer.

9. Sickness Absence

The Employee is entitled to Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) in accordance with prevailing legislation. {{CONTRACTUAL_SICK_PAY_TERMS}}. The Employee must notify their line manager of any absence as soon as reasonably practicable on the first day of absence. A self-certification form is required for absences of up to 7 consecutive calendar days. A GP fit note is required for absences of 8 or more consecutive calendar days.

The first {{PROBATION_PERIOD}} months of employment constitute a probationary period, during which either party may terminate this contract by giving {{PROBATION_NOTICE}} weeks' written notice. The Employer may extend the probationary period by up to a further {{PROBATION_EXTENSION}} months where performance, attendance or conduct requires further assessment.

Following the successful completion of the probationary period, the minimum period of notice required to terminate this contract is:

  • By the Employer: {{EMPLOYER_NOTICE_PERIOD}} (increasing to the statutory minimum under the Employment Rights Act 1996 where this is greater).
  • By the Employee: {{EMPLOYEE_NOTICE_PERIOD}}.

12. Confidentiality

The Employee shall not at any time, whether during or after the termination of employment, disclose to any person any confidential information relating to the Employer's business, clients, suppliers, operations, finances or trade secrets that they may acquire during the course of employment, except as required by law or with the express written consent of the Employer.

This obligation continues after the termination of employment without limit in time, save where the information enters the public domain other than through a breach of this clause.

13. Intellectual Property

All intellectual property rights (including inventions, designs, copyright works, software, databases and trade marks) created by the Employee during the course of their employment and in connection with the Employer's business vest in and belong to the Employer absolutely. The Employee assigns all such rights to the Employer, and agrees to execute such further documents as the Employer may reasonably require to give effect to this clause.

14. Disciplinary and Grievance Procedures

The Employer's disciplinary and grievance procedures are set out in the Employee Handbook, a copy of which will be provided to the Employee on commencement of employment. These procedures do not form part of the Employee's contract of employment. The Employer will comply with the Acas Code of Practice on Disciplinary and Grievance Procedures.

15. Data Protection

The Employee consents to the Employer holding and processing personal data (including sensitive personal data) for the purposes of employment administration, payroll and legal compliance, in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018. A full privacy notice will be provided separately.

16. Governing Law

This contract is governed by the laws of England and Wales. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the Employment Tribunal and the courts of England and Wales in respect of any dispute arising out of or in connection with this contract.
